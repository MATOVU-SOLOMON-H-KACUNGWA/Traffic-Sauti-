Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What should I do if I encounter a pothole on the road?

A: If you encounter a pothole while driving, try to safely maneuver around it to avoid damage to your vehicle. If you believe the pothole poses a significant danger, you can report it to your local authorities or transportation department.

Q: How can I learn more about pothole and traffic safety awareness?

A: For more information on pothole and traffic safety, please visit our partner website at www.unra.go.ug. We provide valuable resources and tips to help you stay safe on the road.

Q: Are there any tips for preventing pothole-related damage to my vehicle?

A: Yes, here are some tips:

  • Drive at a safe speed and maintain a safe following distance to give you more time to react to potholes.
  • Avoid driving through puddles, as they might be concealing potholes.
  • Maintain proper tire pressure and ensure your vehicle's suspension is in good condition.

Q: What should I do if my vehicle is damaged by a pothole?

A: If your vehicle is damaged by a pothole, you can:

  • Contact your insurance company to inquire about coverage for pothole-related damage.
  • Report the pothole to local authorities to prevent further incidents.
